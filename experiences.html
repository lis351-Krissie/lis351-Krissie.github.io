Group Experiences

Exo-K's leader, Suho, was the first member to join SM Entertainment in 2006. ExoK and Exo-M debuted with the single Mama on April 8, 2012, followed by the EP Mama on April 9. The Korean version of the EP peaked at number one on South Korea's Gaon Album Chart and at number eight on the Billboard World Albums Chart, while the Mandarin version reached number two on China's Sina Album Chart and number one on various Chinese streaming platforms.

Exo was awarded Best New Asian Group at the 2012 Mnet Asian Music Awards and the Newcomer Award at the Golden Disc Awards. Their third EP, Overdose, was released on May 7, 2014. By the end of 2014, Exo was Japan's best-selling K-pop artist of the year. On May 15, 2014, Kris filed a lawsuit against SM Entertainment to terminate his contract. On October 10, Luhan also filed a lawsuit against SM Entertainment to terminate his contract, citing health problems and different treatment than the group's Korean members.

In 2016-2017, Exo continued to achieve success with their third studio album, Ex'Act, and its singles Lucky One and Monster . They also released a repackaged edition titled Lotto. The group members spent time advancing their solo careers and Chen, Baekhyun, and Xiumin debuted in the group's first sub-unit, ExoCBX. Lay released his debut EP, Lose Control. Exo's fourth studio album, The War, was released in 2017, and they began their third world tour, Exo Planet #4 The Elyxion. In 2018, Exo released their first Japanese studio album, Countdown, and performed at the closing ceremony of the 2018 Winter Olympics in Pyeongchang. They also released their fifth studio album, Don't Mess Up My Tempo.
